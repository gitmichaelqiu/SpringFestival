THE RED ENVELOPE

Giving red envelopes is a way to share blessings and good luck for the new year. The significance isn't the money inside, but the envelope itself.

The red color symbolizes energy, happiness, and good luck. Wrapping money in red envelopes is expected to bestow more happiness and blessings on the receivers.

MYTHOLOGY

The Eight Immortals Legend

According to legend, a demon named Sui would touch the heads of sleeping children on New Year's Eve, causing them illness. Eight fairies transformed themselves into eight coins to help parents protect their child. They slipped the coins under the child's pillow on red paper. When Sui appeared, flashes of light from the coins frightened the demon away.

THE AWAKENING

Believed to control rain, typhoons, and floods, the mythical dragon is an auspicious symbol deeply admired in Chinese culture.

The Dragon Dance (Wulong) is performed with a long, flexible figure made of wood, bamboo, and fabric. By mimicking the supposed movements of this river spirit in a sinuous, undulating manner, dancers invite good harvest and prosperity.

The Origin

SIGNIFICANCE

The Spring Festival, or Lunar New Year, marks the end of winter and the beginning of the spring season. It's the most important traditional festival in China, deeply rooted in history, honoring deities and ancestors.

THE LEGEND

Legend speaks of Nian, a terrifying beast that lived under the sea or in the mountains. Every New Year's Eve, it would surface to attack villages.

The villagers eventually discovered its three weaknesses: loud noises, bright lights, and the color red. This ancient battle birthed the firecrackers and red decorations we use today.
